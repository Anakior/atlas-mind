# Welcome to Atlas Mind

> **It's your mind, not someone else's.**

You are looking at a **live, fully offline demo** of [Atlas Mind](https://github.com/Anakior/atlas-mind) — a
self-hostable wiki / knowledge-base engine, and an external brain you share with
your AI.

Everything you see is a **single static HTML file** served from GitHub Pages.
No server, no database, no network calls — the entire mind (documents, search
index, backlink graph, fonts and libraries) is embedded in the page you just
loaded. The real engine adds live editing, accounts, share links and an MCP
endpoint for your AI, but the *reading* experience is exactly this.

## Try the viewer

- Hit the **search** box (top of the sidebar) and type `wikilink` or `notion`.
- Open **[[guides/markdown-showcase]]** to see rendering: tables, code, quotes,
  task lists.
- Open the **[[decks/atlas-dashboard|admin dashboard mockup]]** — a full HTML app
  screen (charts, KPIs, table) rendered inline, animations and all.
- Scroll any document to the bottom: **backlinks** show every other note that
  points here.
- Open the graph (`Ctrl/Cmd+G`) to see the whole mind at once — including the
  teal **[[remotes/mira-garden/start-here|mental node]]** this demo follows from
  another atlas.
- Open the **Tasks** view (the checklist icon, top of the sidebar): every
  `- [ ]` from across the mind in one place — start with
  [[guides/getting-started-checklist]].
- Some documents carry **highlighted passages** with margin notes — open
  [[features/own-your-data]] or [[notes/why-not-notion]] and look for the amber
  highlights.
